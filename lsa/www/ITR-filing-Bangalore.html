Form No. : ITR-1 SAHAJ

Form ITR-1 SAHAJ is an income tax return form in India designed for individuals with straightforward income sources. It is commonly used by salaried individuals, pensioners, and those with income from a single house property or other sources like interest and agricultural income below Rs. 5,000. The form requires details of income, deductions, and tax computation. It is a simplified form to facilitate easy filing for individuals with uncomplicated financial scenarios. Taxpayers using ITR-1 should ensure accurate reporting of income and adherence to specified guidelines. Filing ITR-1 SAHAJ is crucial for compliance with Indian tax regulations and helps individuals fulfill their tax obligations efficiently.

Form No. : ITR-2

ITR-2 is an Income Tax Return form used by individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) not having income from business or profession. It is applicable for taxpayers with income from capital gains, house property, and other sources. The form requires details of income, deductions claimed, and taxes paid. Additionally, it mandates disclosure of foreign assets and income for residents with global income. ITR-2 is filed by those not eligible for ITR-1 and includes information on salary, multiple house properties, and investments. Taxpayers must accurately fill the form and submit it online or in paper format, ensuring compliance with income tax regulations.

Form No. : ITR-3

Form ITR-3, also known as Income Tax Return-3, is a document prescribed by the Indian Income Tax Department for individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) having income from business or profession. It is not applicable to individuals earning income through a proprietary business or those opting for presumptive income scheme under sections 44AD and 44AE. ITR-3 requires detailed information about the taxpayer's income, expenses, and deductions related to business or profession. Additionally, it includes details of partner's shares in the firm, making it suitable for those involved in partnership firms. Filing ITR-3 is essential for taxpayers falling under the specified criteria to comply with the income tax regulations in India.

Form No. : ITR-4 Sugam

ITR-4 Sugam is an income tax return form in India designed for individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) with income from a presumptive business or profession. It is a simplified form, facilitating easier tax filing for taxpayers engaged in specified professions or businesses, such as freelancers, small businesses, and professionals. The form requires taxpayers to provide details of their income, deductions, and tax computation. Additionally, ITR-4 Sugam is applicable to those opting for the presumptive taxation scheme under sections 44AD, 44ADA, and 44AE of the Income Tax Act. By using this form, eligible taxpayers can fulfill their tax obligations efficiently, benefiting from a streamlined process tailored for their specific financial activities.

Form No. : ITR-5

Form ITR-5 is an income tax return form used by Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs), firms, Association of Persons (AOPs), and Body of Individuals (BOIs) that are not claiming exemption under Section 11. It is filed with the Income Tax Department of India to declare their income, gains, and losses for a particular financial year. The form requires detailed information about the taxpayer's income, deductions, and tax payments. Additionally, it mandates the disclosure of partner details in the case of a partnership firm. Filing ITR-5 ensures compliance with tax regulations and facilitates the assessment of tax liability. Taxpayers must submit this form by the specified due date to avoid penalties or legal consequences.

Form No. : ITR-6

Form ITR-6 is an income tax return form in India, primarily applicable to companies that do not claim exemption under Section 11 of the Income Tax Act. It is filed by entities such as companies, including those claiming deductions under sections 11 and 10AA, engaged in charitable or religious activities. This form requires comprehensive details regarding income, losses, deductions, and taxes paid by the company during the financial year. It includes information about the business's nature, income sources, and financial particulars. Additionally, ITR-6 mandates the disclosure of details related to audit reports, balance sheets, and profit and loss statements. Accurate and timely submission of Form ITR-6 is crucial for complying with Indian income tax regulations.

Form No. : ITR-7

Form ITR-7 is an income tax return form in India, primarily designed for individuals and entities that fall under the category of Association of Persons (AOP), Body of Individuals (BOI), firms, and companies. This form is filed under Section 139(4A), 139(4B), 139(4C), and 139(4D) of the Income Tax Act. ITR-7 is an annexure-less form, meaning that no additional documents need to be attached while filing. It requires detailed information about the taxpayer's income, deductions, and tax computation. The form includes sections for personal details, income details, and details of the tax computation. It is crucial for those falling under the specified categories to accurately fill and submit ITR-7 to comply with income tax regulations in India.
